Anthropology

Born and raised on the tiny - beautiful, but TINY - island of O'ahu, I always wanted to see and learn about the big world beyond the shores of Hawai'i. I thus earned my BA (University of Hawai'i at Manoa, 2010) and MA (Harvard University, 2013) in Anthropology.

As part of my research, I traveled to (and lived in) Japan, Australia, and across the US. I have worked on ethnographic projects ranging from consumer perceptions of genetically modified crops to a local folk legend about the death of Jesus Christ in Japan (yes, Japan).

Check out Anthropology for more information about my research.

UX Research and Design

In my current role as Coordinator of Publications and Programs at the Mahindra Humanities Center at Harvard, I manage the digital presence of the Center (as well as their 35 seminars and 2 fellowship programs). As I learned more about web development and design, I fell in love with the concept of "human-centered design."

I now aspire to apply my skills toward a career in user experience (UX) research and design.
